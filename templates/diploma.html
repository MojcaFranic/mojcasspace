TEMPORARY MODULAR UNIT IN CASE OF NATURAL DISASTER

Diploma; Final Thesis

In times of climate change and ever more frequent natural disasters it is important that we are well prepared for such extreme events. Current evacuation plans include housing people in tents, trailers and containers, which are not suitable for all weather and climate conditions, or are too big for optimal storage. The purpose of this thesis is to find the best possible solution for all these problems. A temporary modular unit has to be small enough for optimal storage and transportation, at the same time it has to be suitable for use in any season in Slovenia. It may happen, that people have to stay in temporary camps for a longer period of time. That is why the goal of designing this unit is to offer the user the most comfortable stay possible. Because of its modularity, the unit is suitable for different types of households, so it does not focus on a typical four member family. Elements of the modular construction system of the company Trimo are used in the design of the unit. This system has pre-planned and inbuilt installations, so we can easily integrate the unit in a temporary camp that already has a local source of energy.

Due to the diverse composition of residential communities, I wanted to adapt the modular units to as many users as possible. By adding modules to basic structures, we can gain space for more people. By doing so, families and communities that are accustomed to living in the same household, do not have to be separated in difficult situations.


For inspiration I started to play with playdough and came up with an idea of cubuses that can be put together regarding the need. I wanted to design something simple and functional, yet attractive.


Each base unit is meant for two people and consists of three modules. The internal dimensions of the module are 220 cm x 220 cm. A set of three modules consists of an entrance hall with a small kitchen, a living room and a bedroom for two people. The basic modules can be placed in two ways, one in the form of a letter I, the other in the shape of a letter L. We can add up to two modules on each basis. Those modules are identical sleeping departments as the one in the base. An additional spatial unit can be added to the same level, one floor higher or both. That gives us the option for a unit for a maximum of two, four or six pople.


I used company Trimo's modular system to design my units. Each module consists of a load-bearing steel structure of the floor, which is welded in one piece. This consists of angular projections and interfaces on the edges. On all four edges, the battens are made of bent steel. The pillars on the upper part are screwed into the supporting structure of the roof, which is very similar to the construction of the floor and is also in one piece.

The unit allows illumination with natural light. At each of the rooms, at least half of one wall is glazed over the entire length of the panel. Shading is arranged with sliding panels from sheet metal on the outside. Besides shading, the panels allow privacy, since they obscure the view from the outside. Since all materials are flexible - steel construction, sheet metal on the outside of the walls and sliding panels - the colors of a single unit can be unified. That way the unit, especially with cladding panels, looks like a tetris cube at first glance. Vivid colors and shapes bring an optimistic touch to the crowded settlement.

Below I showed a view onto the facade from front, right, back and left side. All of the views are a sample from basic unit I with 2 additional modules.

The unit's floors are covered with a PVC floor covering as the material is light to transport and has economic and hygienic properties. To make the unit more homey, we can select a pattern that imitates a warm floor. Walls are covered with a sheet metal or chipboard. Small kitchen has a cooker with two plates and a small sink.

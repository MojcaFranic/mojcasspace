SUMMER HOUSE IN THE MOUNTAINS

The goal of the second year studio in interior design was to create a micro house with maximal limit of 16 m2. I decided to design a shelter in the mountains in Gorenjska region in Slovenia. Since it is meant to be located in the middle of the woods, I wanted to integrate the object into its surroundings. For an inspiration I took a wooden log and started sketching from there.

The summer house is meant to be a shelter for weekend trips and hikes in the highlands as it is a custom in Slovenia. Alpinizm and active spare time is a big part of our culture. The space only offers us a place to stay overnight, prepare a quick meal and take care of basic hygiene.


Entering into the shelter we find ourselves in a small kitchen with a table and two chairs. In the space in the back is an inbuilt bunk bed for two people. Next to sleeping space is a seperate wash room with a sink, small shower and toilet.


There is no electricity or running water. As a water source I placed a cystern in the roof of the bathroom where rain water would be stored. Drop from over-head storage should be enough for the shower and sink. Water used for washing would be additionally recycled to flush the compost toilet. In the kitchen I placed an old time favourite oven called "Gašperček". It works as a wood burner and is used for heating as well as acting as an iron stove-top.

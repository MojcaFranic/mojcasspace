FURSPRAY

Dog Grooming Salon

In third year interior design studio we were given blueprints of ground level of one of the oldest shops in Ljubljana. Our task was to use the space to design a shop, a bar and a hair studio. I decided to use a part of the space for a dog grooming salon.


I started with designing the space to it's maximal potential to be functional and welcoming for the customers. To give workers some privacy I only left the enterance covered in windows. People enter the salon into a reception with a waiting room. Beyond reception there is a grooming space for furry customers. In the back is a storage with some cages for the dogs to wait in case their owners haven't returned yet.

There are some frontal views of the salon:

I named the salon "Furspray". For the logotype I used a silhouette of a spaniel and turned it's ears upside down, to make it seem as a result of hairspray.


For the furniture I imagined to recycle some older pieces. Wood would be treated with fresh coat of black or white paint and cushions refreshed with pink and purple fabrics. I used chandeliers to accompany the modernized barrock style.

Pictures below show waiting room for the owners where they can read a magazine and grab a coffee while waiting for their pets. Behind reception is the grooming space with profesional bathtubes and tables.
